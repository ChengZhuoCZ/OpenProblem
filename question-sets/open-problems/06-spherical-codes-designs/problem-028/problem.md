# Problem 028 - Coulomb-Thomson and Energy Minimization Problems on Spheres

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

For a fixed \(N\), among all \(N\)-point subsets \(X=\{x_1,\ldots,x_N\}\subset S^{n-1}\), minimize the Coulomb energy

\[
\sum_{1\le i<j\le N}\frac{1}{\|x_i-x_j\|}.
\]

Determine both the minimum value and the structure of minimizers. More generally, study analogous minimization problems for other energy functions.

## Background

This is the Coulomb-Thomson viewpoint on spherical point configurations, complementary to the Tammes maximum-separation problem.

## Known Context

- The problem is classical and has many variants under Riesz and other pair potentials.
- The lecture presents it as a broad coding-theoretic problem on spheres.

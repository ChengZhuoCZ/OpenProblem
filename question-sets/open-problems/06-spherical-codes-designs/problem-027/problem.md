# Problem 027 - Few-Distance Sets in General Metric and Algebraic Spaces

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Extend the theory of \(s\)-distance sets beyond spheres and Euclidean spaces to broader metric spaces or real algebraic sets. Determine useful upper bounds, extremal examples, and classification results in these settings.

## Background

The lecture marks this as a possible research direction and points to work by Petrov-Pohoata and by Hegedus-Ronyai on few-distance sets and algebraic sets.

## Known Context

- Classical polynomial-method bounds apply in Euclidean settings.
- Algebraic constraints can change the effective dimension and available bounds.

# Problem 023 - Kissing Numbers in Unsolved Dimensions

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

For each dimension \(n\), determine the kissing number \(k(n)\): the maximum size of \(X\subset S^{n-1}\) with pairwise Euclidean distance at least \(1\), equivalently angular distance at least \(\pi/3\), equivalently inner product at most \(1/2\).

## Background

The lecture lists the known exact values

\[
k(2)=6,\quad k(3)=12,\quad k(4)=24,\quad k(8)=240,\quad k(24)=196560,
\]

and notes that other dimensions remain open.

## Known Context

- Dimensions \(8\) and \(24\) are tied to the \(E_8\) and Leech lattice configurations.
- The problem is a central spherical code extremal problem.

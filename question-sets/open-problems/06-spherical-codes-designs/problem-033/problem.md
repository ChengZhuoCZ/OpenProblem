# Problem 033 - Classification of Tight Spherical \(t\)-Designs for \(t=4,5,7\)

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Classify tight spherical \(t\)-designs on \(S^{n-1}\), especially in the remaining open cases

\[
t\in\{4,5,7\}.
\]

## Background

The Fisher-type lower bound gives a notion of tightness for spherical designs. Bannai-Damerell restricts possible tight strengths to

\[
t\in\{1,2,3,4,5,7,11\}
\]

for \(n\ge3\). The cases \(t=1,2,3,11\) are classified, while \(t=4,5,7\) remain open with additional nonexistence results known.

## Known Context

- \(t=1\): antipodal pair.
- \(t=2\): regular simplex.
- \(t=3\): cross-polytope.
- \(t=11\): the Leech lattice configuration in dimension \(24\), unique.
- For \(t=4,5,7\), only a small number of examples are known and strong dimension restrictions apply.

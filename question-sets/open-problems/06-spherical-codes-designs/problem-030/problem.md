# Problem 030 - Large Spherical \(t\)-Designs from Finite Group Orbits and Lattice Shells

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Many explicit spherical \(t\)-designs arise either as:

1. an orbit of a finite group \(G\subset O(n)\), or
2. a shell of a lattice \(L\subset\mathbb R^n\).

Determine whether the possible design strength \(t\) in each construction family is bounded by an absolute constant independent of \(n\).

## Background

The lecture states that known examples from finite group orbits have \(t\le19\) for \(n\ge3\), and known examples from lattice shells have \(t\le11\).

## Known Context

- This problem concerns explicit and highly structured constructions, not abstract existence.
- It is tied to finite group representations, lattices, and association schemes.

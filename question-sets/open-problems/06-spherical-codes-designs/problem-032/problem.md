# Problem 032 - Existence Size of Spherical \(t\)-Designs When \(t\) Is Fixed and Dimension Grows

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Understand the optimal existence bounds for spherical \(t\)-designs on \(S^{n-1}\) in the regime where \(t\) is fixed and \(n\to\infty\).

## Background

Seymour-Zaslavsky proved existence of spherical \(t\)-designs for all \(n\) and \(t\). Bondarenko-Radchenko-Viazovska proved asymptotically optimal order in the regime where \(n\) is fixed and \(t\to\infty\). The lecture asks about the complementary regime with \(t\) fixed and dimension growing.

## Known Context

- For fixed \(n\), designs of size \(O_n(t^{n-1})\) exist.
- The high-dimensional fixed-strength regime requires different asymptotic tracking.

# Problem 026 - Extremal \(s\)-Inner Product Sets in Euclidean Space

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Call \(X\subset\mathbb R^n\) an \(s\)-inner product set if the set

\[
\{x\cdot y : x,y\in X,\ x\ne y\}
\]

has size \(s\). It is known that

\[
|X|\le {n+s\choose s}.
\]

Find examples or prove nonexistence of equality cases for \(s\ge2\), \(n\ge2\). More generally, characterize extremal \(s\)-inner product sets.

## Background

The lecture records the Deza-Frankl and Nozaki context and notes that no equality example is known for \(s\ge2\), \(n\ge2\).

## Known Context

- The \(s=1\) case includes regular-simplex-type behavior but allows more general configurations when points are not constrained to one sphere.
- The lecture asks for examples in small dimensions and for characterization of \(1\)-inner product sets with \(|X|=n+1\).

## Data Still Needed

- Clarify whether equality can occur when points lie on multiple concentric spheres.
- Separate \(s=1\) classification from the \(s\ge2\) existence problem.

# Problem 025 - Maximum \(s\)-Distance Sets in Euclidean Space

## Source

This is taken from Eiichi Bannai's G2C2 Lecture No. 1 on spherical \(t\)-designs.

## Problem Statement

Let \(X\subset \mathbb R^n\) be an \(s\)-distance set. The general upper bound is

\[
|X|\le {n+s\choose s}.
\]

Classify the equality cases and determine whether there are any examples beyond the known \(s=1\) simplex case and the known \(s=2,n=8\) example.

## Background

The lecture notes that one equality example for \(s=2\) with \(|X|={n+2\choose 2}\) is known for \(n=8\), due to Lisoněk, and that no other example with equality is known for \(s\ge2\), \(n\ge2\).

## Known Context

- For \(s=1\), equality gives a regular simplex.
- The lecture mentions recent classification progress for maximum two-distance sets in low dimensions.

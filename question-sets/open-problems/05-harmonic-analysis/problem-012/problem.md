# Problem 012 - A Multi-Frequency Carleson Problem

## Source

This is Problem 7 from "Some Problems in Harmonic Analysis", contributed by Ben Krause.

## Problem Statement

For \(K\) one-separated frequencies \(\{\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_K\}\), determine the sharp growth in \(K\) of the corresponding multi-frequency Carleson operator from the source.

A key target is whether, for \(1<p<\infty\), one can prove bounds of the form

\[
\|C\|_{L^p\to L^p}\lesssim_{\varepsilon,p} K^\varepsilon
\]

for every \(\varepsilon>0\).

## Background

The problem arose in work on a discrete Carleson operator along the primes. The known \(L^2\) argument uses Bourgain-type ideas and variational Carleson estimates.

## Known Context

- The source records logarithmic growth in \(L^2\).
- Away from \(L^2\), known estimates lose powers of \(K\).
- Nearly \(K^\varepsilon\) growth would improve the range of applications to discrete Carleson theorems along primes.

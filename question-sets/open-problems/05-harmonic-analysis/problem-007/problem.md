# Problem 007 - Generalized Additive Energy on the Two-Sphere

## Source

This is Problem 2 from "Some Problems in Harmonic Analysis", contributed by Ciprian Demeter.

## Problem Statement

For a finite set \(A \subset \mathbb R^d\), define the generalized additive energy

\[
E_n(A)=\#\{(a_1,\ldots,a_{2n})\in A^{2n}:a_1+\cdots+a_n=a_{n+1}+\cdots+a_{2n}\}.
\]

Prove or disprove that, for every \(\varepsilon>0\),

\[
E_2(A)\lesssim_\varepsilon (\#A)^{2+\varepsilon}
\]

whenever \(A\subset S^2\).

## Background

The corresponding estimate is known for subsets of the two-dimensional paraboloid through decoupling methods.

## Known Context

- The target exponent is near the minimal scale expected for curved surfaces.
- The problem asks whether spherical curvature gives comparable additive-energy control to the paraboloid setting.
